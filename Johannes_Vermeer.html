Girl with a Pearl Earring

Girl with a Pearl Earring, painted by Dutch artist Johannes Vermeer around 1665, is one of the most beloved and recognizable works of the Dutch Golden Age. Often referred to as the “Mona Lisa of the North,” the painting depicts a young woman wearing an exotic turban and a striking, oversized pearl earring.

The composition is simple yet captivating — the subject is set against a dark, plain background, which brings full attention to her expressive gaze and luminous skin. Vermeer’s masterful use of light and color creates a sense of intimacy, as if the viewer has caught a fleeting, private moment. The soft shadows and subtle reflections on the pearl show his exceptional technical skill.

Today, the painting is housed in the Mauritshuis museum in The Hague, Netherlands. While little is known about the identity of the girl, her image has become an icon of timeless beauty and intrigue, inspiring countless adaptations, novels, and films.
